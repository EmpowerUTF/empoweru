Framework

UTF shows you two things other systems miss: how your mind is wired, and what your current season actually needs. Not who you should be. Just who you are, and where you are right now.

The Architecture

COM: Cognitive Operating Mode

Your stable cognitive identity. How you naturally think, decide, and relate to people. This doesn't change with your mood or circumstances. It's how you're wired.

21 types across 9 dimensions + the E axis

SOM: Seasonal Operating Mode

Your current state. How much energy you have, what you're focused on, and what your system actually needs right now. This changes as your life changes.

9 modes across a 3×3 grid

Together, COM + SOM = your UTF profile. Example: You might be a Composer (quality-obsessed, detail-driven, independent) in a Driver season (high energy, output-focused). That combination tells you this is a rare window where your perfectionism can actually produce. Set deadlines and ship. No other system gives you both layers.

What makes UTF different from MBTI, Big Five, and other systems?

▼

Most personality systems give you a fixed label and stop there. MBTI sorts you into one of 16 boxes. Big Five gives you five scores but no actionable guidance. Enneagram describes your motivation but not your current capacity. None of them tell you what to do differently this month.

UTF measures your stable cognitive wiring (COM) AND your current seasonal state (SOM) as two separate, interacting layers. This means it can tell you not just how you think, but whether your current season is aligned with your wiring or working against it, and what to do about it.

We also measure on continuous dimensions, not binary switches. You can be high on both logic and empathy, both structure and flexibility. Real people don't fit in either/or boxes, and UTF doesn't try to force them.

The Science Behind It

How does UTF measure cognitive patterns?

▼

Dimensional Measurement

COM measures 9 cognitive dimensions: pattern thinking, decision speed, systems thinking, structure preference, social reading, quality focus, people priority, independence, and emotional stability. These shape your type. The E axis (social energy) runs alongside them as a separate inward/outward orientation that colors how your type expresses. Your type emerges from the combination of all 9, not from answering a handful of binary questions.

21 Types, Not Buckets

Each COM type represents a distinct cognitive profile, a real pattern that shows up when you measure cognition across multiple dimensions simultaneously. These aren't arbitrary labels. Each type has specific dimension signatures, gate rules, and distance targets that are mathematically validated.

Forced-Choice Seasonal Measurement

SOM uses 27 forced-choice questions where each option maps to one of 5 seasonal dimensions: energy, recovery need, output focus, people focus, and change focus. The format eliminates acquiescence bias and measures what you're actually doing, not what sounds good.

Two-Layer Architecture

COM measures stable traits. SOM measures current state. The synthesis layer computes interactions between them: alignment, tension, burnout risk, and season-appropriate coaching. This is the only personality system that produces both layers and connects them.

Atlas: your cognitive advisor

▼

Atlas is what happens when your UTF profile meets any AI. When you activate Atlas on the synthesis page, it generates an Atlas you paste into any AI chat: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, whatever you use. The AI then knows how you think, what your season needs, and how to talk to you.

It's not a separate app. It's a portable profile that makes any AI smarter about you. The AI adapts its tone, advice, examples, and priorities to match your actual wiring and current state. No generic advice. No one-size-fits-all.

Everything stays private. You paste it yourself. Nothing is sent anywhere automatically. You control what any AI knows about you.
